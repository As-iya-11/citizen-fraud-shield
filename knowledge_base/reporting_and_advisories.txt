OFFICIAL REPORTING CHANNELS AND ADVISORIES (INDIA)

National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:
- Website: cybercrime.gov.in
- Helpline: 1930 (toll-free, national cybercrime helpline)
- Use this to report financial fraud, digital arrest scams, phishing, and other cyber
  crimes. Immediate reporting (within a few hours) significantly improves the chance of
  freezing transferred funds before they are withdrawn by the fraudster.

What to do immediately after a suspected fraud transaction:
1. Call 1930 or report on cybercrime.gov.in as soon as possible — speed matters, since
   funds can be frozen at the receiving bank if reported quickly.
2. Note down: the caller's number, any UPI ID / bank account the money was sent to,
   screenshots of the chat or call, and the exact time of transaction.
3. Inform your bank's fraud helpline to request a freeze on the transaction if still
   possible.
4. File a complaint at the nearest police station if the online portal is not accessible.

Government advisory principles (paraphrased from MHA / RBI / TRAI public advisories):
- Law enforcement and government departments (CBI, ED, Customs, Police, Courts) do not
  conduct investigations, verifications, or arrests over phone or video call.
- Banks and RBI never ask customers to reveal OTP, PIN, CVV, or install remote-access
  software to "verify" an account.
- TRAI does not deactivate mobile numbers over a phone call; any SIM deactivation notice
  received this way is fraudulent.
- Any request to keep a conversation secret from family or authorities is itself a strong
  indicator of fraud.

Reporting counterfeit currency:
- Suspected fake notes should be handed over to the nearest bank branch or police station;
  do not attempt to circulate or use suspected counterfeit currency.
