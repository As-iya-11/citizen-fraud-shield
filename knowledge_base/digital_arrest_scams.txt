DIGITAL ARREST SCAM — PATTERN REFERENCE

What it is:
A "digital arrest" scam is a fraud where callers impersonate law enforcement or government
officials (CBI, ED, Customs, RBI, Police, TRAI, Cyber Crime Cell) and claim the victim is
under investigation for a serious crime (money laundering, drug parcel, SIM card misuse,
tax evasion). Victims are pressured to stay on a video call continuously — sometimes for
hours or days — and are told they are under "digital arrest" and cannot leave, contact
family, or hang up until they pay a "clearance" or "verification" amount.

Common red flags:
1. Caller claims to be from CBI, ED, Customs, TRAI, RBI, or local police and demands you
   stay on a video call.
2. Caller shows a fake ID card, fake arrest warrant, or fake government office backdrop.
3. Victim is told a parcel/courier in their name contains drugs, fake passports, or
   contraband, and that they must "verify" their identity and finances.
4. Victim is isolated — told not to disconnect the call, not to tell family, not to leave
   the room, sometimes for many hours.
5. Urgency and threats: "your accounts will be frozen," "you will be arrested within the
   hour," "this is your last chance to cooperate."
6. Request to transfer money to a "RBI verification account" or "government scrutiny
   account" for a background check, with a promise the money will be refunded after
   verification.
7. Caller ID may be spoofed to look like a genuine government number.
8. No genuine government agency conducts arrests, investigations, or fund verification over
   video call or WhatsApp. No agency asks for money to "verify" your innocence.

Escalation timeline typically seen in real cases:
- Initial contact via phone call or WhatsApp video call, often claiming a courier/SIM
  card issue.
- Rapid escalation within minutes to threat of arrest.
- Sustained video call to prevent victim from seeking outside advice (this is the
  "digital arrest" itself).
- Multiple transfers, sometimes across several hours, until victim's funds are drained or
  family intervenes.

What a citizen should do:
- Real police, CBI, ED, or courts never conduct investigations, verification, or arrests
  over phone/video call.
- Disconnect immediately. Do not transfer any money under threat.
- Verify independently by calling the department's official published number (not any
  number given by the caller).
- Report to the National Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930, or online at cybercrime.gov.in.
- Inform a trusted family member or the local police station in person.
